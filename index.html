Short History Of Romania

Brief History of Romania:

  • 7th century BC - Greek colonies established on the Black Sea coast in the 7th century BC became important centres of commerce with the local tribes.
  • 46 AD - The Romans reached Dacia during Burebista's reign and conquered Dobruja.
  • 85 AD - Dacia was again united under Decebalus around 85 AD. He resisted the Romans for decades, but the Roman army defeated his troops in 106 AD.
  • 271 AD - Emperor Aurelian ordered the evacuation of the province Dacia Trajana.
  • 376 AD - The Huns invaded the Dacian territory, causing new waves of migrations.
  • 454 AD - The Gepids took possession of the former Dacia province.
  • 570 AD - The nomadic Avars defeated the Gepids and established a powerful empire.
  • 680 AD - The Bulgars, who also came from the Eurasian steppes, occupied the Lower Danube region.
  • 790 AD - The Avar Khaganate collapsed, Bulgaria became the dominant power of the region, occupying lands as far as the river Tisa.
  • 1310 - Basarab I of Wallachia united the Romanian polities between the southern Carpathians and the Lower Danube. He defeated the Hungarian royal army in the Battle of Posada and secured the independence of Wallachia in 1330.
  • 1594 - The princes of Transylvania, Wallachia, and Moldavia joined the Holy League against the Ottoman Empire.
  • 1600 - The Wallachian prince, Michael the Brave, united the three principalities under his rule
  • 1821 - Taking advantage of the Greek War of Independence, a Wallachian lesser nobleman, Tudor Vladimirescu, stirred up a revolt against the Ottomans, but he was murdered by Phanariot Greeks.
  • 1848 - Mihail Kogălniceanu, Nicolae Bălcescu and other leaders of the 1848 revolutions in Moldavia and Wallachia demanded the emancipation of the peasants and the union of the two principalities, but Russian and Ottoman troops crushed their revolt. The Wallachian revolutionists were the first to adopt the blue, yellow and red tricolour as the national flag.
  • 1859 - Alexandru Ioan Cuza became through election the ruling prince of Moldavia and Wallachia.
  • 1862 - The united principalities officially adopted the name Romania.
  • 1914 - The country remained neutral when World War I broke out, but Prime Minister Ion I. C. Brătianu started negotiations with the Entente Powers.
  • 1941 - Romania entered World War II soon after the German invasion of the Soviet Union.
  • 1944 - Romania switched sides during the war, and nearly 250,000 Romanian troops joined the Red Army's military campaign against Hungary and Germany.
  • 1947 - The Communist Party forced King Michael I to abdicate and leave the country and proclaimed Romania a people's republic.
  • 1989 - Romanian Revolution of December 1989 in which thousands were killed or injured.
  • 2004 - Romania joins NATO, as a result of developing closer ties with Western Europe and the United States.
  • 2007 - The country applied in June 1993 for membership in the European Union and became an Associated State of the EU in 1995, an Acceding Country in 2004, and a full member on 1 January 2007.
Fun Fact About Romania
Did you know that according to the Guinness World Records the heaviest building in the world is in Romania? The Palace of Parliament in Bucharest contains 700,000 tonnes of steel and bronze, 1,000,000 square feet of marble, 3500 tonnes of crystal, and 900,000 square meters of wood. The Palace is also the second-largest administrative building in the world (after the Pentagon), encompassing 365,000 square meters (3,930,000 sq ft) and 1,100 rooms.
